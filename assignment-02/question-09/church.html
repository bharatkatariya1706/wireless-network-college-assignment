Description of White Church

White Church

Indore is the largest city in Madhya Pradesh. It is ranked as the cleanest city in the country. It is a city with a perfect blend of cultural heritage and modernity. Indore is also famous for its food culture. It has a Maharashtrian, Rajasthani, and Gujarathi blend in its food. The Indori street food is also renowned. Indore is also famous for its architecture. It has many such architectural marvels including palaces and temples. White Church also known as St Ann’s Church was established on 9th August 1858 under British Raj. White Church in Indore is a religious revelation and European architecture. This white beauty is painted white and is surrounded by trees around.
White Church in Indore is the oldest church in Central India. The history of the White Church dates back to 1858 when it was built under the British Raj. It was officially founded on 9 August 1858. These architectural marvels respect European-style architecture. It was established by the Protestant community of Indore for the Christians residing in India and Army personnel who visited the country during those days. Originally it was called St. Ann’s Church, but later it was well-known as a white Church because of the White colour edifice. The architect of this White Church in Indore was Engineer Ross McMohan and was built under the leadership of the former Governor General of the British government in India. The white edifice is surrounded by lush green trees. The European style of architecture of Indore White Church is what makes it one of the most beautiful places to visit in Indore, Indore White Church was built with the help of white stone and marble. The white crucification is erected on the top of the church, The Glass with a Portrait of Jesus.
